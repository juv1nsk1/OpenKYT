Below is a **structured glossary of blockchain-related financial crime and money laundering terminology**, designed with both operational and research depth in mind (patterns, actors, typologies, and behavioral signals). This should be useful for analytical modeling, compliance architecture, and academic framing.

---

# 🔎 Glossary of Blockchain Crimes & AML Terminology

---

## A

### **Address Poisoning**

A deception tactic where attackers send small amounts of crypto from addresses visually similar to a victim’s past transaction counterparties, hoping the victim mistakenly copies and reuses the attacker’s address.

**Pattern signals:**

* Low-value inbound transfers
* Similar address prefix/suffix to known counterparties
* No prior interaction graph relationship

---

### **Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) Groups**

State-sponsored or state-aligned hacking organizations conducting long-term cyber operations, often including crypto theft.

**Example Actor:** Lazarus Group (North Korea)

---

### **Anti-Money Laundering (AML)**

Regulatory and technological frameworks designed to detect, prevent, and report illicit financial flows.

In blockchain contexts, AML often involves:

* Transaction monitoring
* Wallet risk scoring
* Behavioral clustering
* On-chain forensic tracing

---

## B

### **Blockchain Forensics**

Analytical methodologies used to trace illicit funds across public ledgers using graph analysis, heuristics, clustering, and behavioral profiling.

---

### **Bridge Exploit**

Attack targeting cross-chain bridges to steal locked assets.

**Pattern signals:**

* Sudden large outflows from bridge contracts
* Exploitation transaction preceding drain event
* Rapid laundering through mixers and cross-chain swaps

---

## C

### **Chain Hopping**

Rapid conversion of funds across multiple blockchains to complicate tracing.

**Pattern signals:**

* Transfers through bridges or swap protocols
* Short dwell time between chains
* Fragmented transaction paths

---

### **Clustering (Wallet Clustering)**

Heuristic grouping of addresses likely controlled by the same entity.

Common heuristics:

* Multi-input transaction heuristic
* Change address detection
* Behavioral similarity metrics

---

### **CoinJoin**

Privacy-enhancing technique where multiple users combine transactions to obfuscate fund origin.

**Pattern signals:**

* Equal output amounts
* Coordinated multi-party inputs
* Structured denomination outputs

---

### **Compliance Evasion Infrastructure**

Layered laundering architecture including mixers, shell wallets, OTC brokers, and mule accounts.

---

## D

### **Darknet Markets (DNMs)**

Online marketplaces operating on Tor or similar networks, accepting cryptocurrency for illicit goods/services.

**Pattern signals:**

* Regular mid-sized inbound transfers from retail wallets
* Consolidation into high-volume treasury wallets
* Interaction with known DNM clusters

---

### **DeFi Exploit**

Smart contract vulnerability used to drain funds.

**Pattern signals:**

* Flash loan usage
* Abnormal contract calls
* Immediate laundering through DEX swaps

---

### **Dusting Attack**

Sending tiny amounts of cryptocurrency to multiple wallets to analyze subsequent movement and deanonymize users.

---

## E

### **Exit Scam**

Fraud where project founders disappear with investor funds (common in ICOs, NFT mints, DeFi protocols).

**Pattern signals:**

* Liquidity pool drain
* Sudden admin key transfer
* Treasury wallet emptied to new addresses

---

## F

### **Flash Loan Attack**

Uncollateralized loan used within a single transaction to manipulate price or exploit vulnerabilities.

Often used in DeFi protocol exploits.

---

## H

### **Hawala (Crypto-Enabled Informal Value Transfer)**

Off-chain settlement system using crypto as bridging asset.

**Pattern signals:**

* Repeated bilateral transfers
* High velocity, low profit margin spreads
* Interaction with high-risk jurisdictions

---

## L

### **Layering (Money Laundering Stage 2)**

Complex movement of funds to obscure origin after placement.

On-chain manifestations:

* Splitting into many wallets
* Use of mixers
* Cross-chain swaps
* NFT wash transactions

---

### **Lazarus Group**

North Korean state-sponsored hacking organization responsible for major crypto thefts.

**Notable incidents:**

* Ronin Bridge hack (2022)
* Harmony Horizon Bridge hack
* Multiple exchange breaches

**Behavioral laundering pattern:**

1. Initial consolidation
2. Splitting into smaller wallets
3. Mixing (Tornado Cash historically)
4. Chain hopping (ETH → BTC via swaps)
5. OTC cash-out via Asia-based brokers

**Key traits:**

* Long dormancy periods
* Structured laundering phases
* Use of DeFi infrastructure

---

## M

### **Mixer (Tumblers)**

Services that obfuscate transaction history by pooling and redistributing funds.

Examples:

* Tornado Cash (Ethereum)
* Wasabi (Bitcoin CoinJoin)

**Pattern signals:**

* Fixed denomination deposits
* Time-delayed withdrawals
* Large anonymity sets

---

### **Money Mule**

Individual knowingly or unknowingly transferring illicit funds on behalf of criminals.

On-chain behavior:

* Receives high-risk funds
* Rapid transfer to exchange
* Low wallet age

---

## O

### **OTC Broker (High-Risk)**

Over-the-counter intermediaries facilitating large crypto trades, sometimes used to convert illicit funds into fiat.

---

## P

### **Peel Chain**

A laundering technique where funds are slowly “peeled” off from a large balance across sequential transactions.

**Pattern signals:**

* Repeated outgoing transactions of slightly smaller value
* Single dominant input source
* Linear chain structure

---

### **Placement (Money Laundering Stage 1)**

Initial introduction of illicit funds into the financial system.

In crypto:

* Deposit to exchange
* Swap into stablecoins
* Use in DeFi lending

---

## R

### **Ransomware**

Malware encrypting victim data in exchange for crypto payment.

**Pattern signals:**

* Many inbound transactions with identical ransom amounts
* Consolidation wallet behavior
* Interaction with mixers post-collection

---

### **Rug Pull**

DeFi or NFT scam where liquidity is withdrawn by creators.

**Pattern signals:**

* LP token withdrawal
* Admin privilege misuse
* Immediate sell-off of token supply

---

## S

### **Sanctions Evasion**

Use of crypto to bypass international financial restrictions.

**Pattern signals:**

* Interaction with OFAC-listed wallets
* Jurisdictional clustering
* Stablecoin usage with offshore OTC desks

---

### **Smurfing (Structuring)**

Breaking large transfers into smaller amounts to avoid detection thresholds.

On-chain analog:

* High-frequency sub-threshold transfers
* Multiple exchange deposit addresses

---

### **Stablecoin Laundering**

Conversion of volatile crypto into USDT/USDC to stabilize value before cash-out.

Often observed in:

* Exchange hacks
* DeFi exploits
* State-sponsored thefts

---

## T

### **Terrorist Financing**

Funding extremist groups via cryptocurrency.

**Pattern signals:**

* Small recurring retail donations
* Public fundraising addresses
* Rapid forwarding to operational wallets

---

### **Transaction Graph Obfuscation**

Use of:

* Mixers
* Chain hopping
* NFT wash trading
* DEX swaps
* Privacy coins

---

## W

### **Wash Trading**

Artificial transaction volume to simulate activity or launder funds.

On-chain signals:

* Repeated buy/sell between related wallets
* Minimal net position change
* High gas-to-volume ratio

---

# 💰 Classical Money Laundering Framework (Applied to Blockchain)

| Stage       | Traditional Finance   | Blockchain Equivalent                    |
| ----------- | --------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Placement   | Cash deposit          | Exchange deposit, DEX swap               |
| Layering    | Shell companies       | Mixers, chain hopping, peel chains       |
| Integration | Legitimate investment | OTC cash-out, real-world asset purchases |

# 🧠 Behavioral Pattern Taxonomy (For Modeling)

You may find this classification useful for feature engineering:

### 1. Flow-Based Features

* In/out degree
* Flow centrality
* Transaction entropy
* Dwell time

### 2. Structural Features

* Clustering coefficient
* Motif detection (peel chains, star patterns)
* Community detection

### 3. Temporal Features

* Burstiness
* Dormancy period
* Time-to-mix

### 4. Counterparty Risk Features

* % interaction with sanctioned wallets
* Exposure to mixers
* Bridge interaction frequency
